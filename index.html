Badminton

What is Badminton?

Badminton is a racquet sport played on a rectangular court divided by a net. Players use racquets to hit a shuttlecock (also called a birdie) over the net. The goal is to land the shuttlecock on the opponent's side of the court and prevent them from returning it.

Types of Games:

  • Singles: One player on each side of the court.
  • Doubles: Two players on each side of the court.
  • Mixed Doubles: One male and one female player on each side of the court.

Equipment:

  • Racquet: Lightweight frame strung with nylon or synthetic gut.
  • Shuttlecock: A small cone-shaped object with feathers or a synthetic skirt.
  • Net: Divides the court in half.
  • Court: Rectangular with specific dimensions for singles and doubles.

Basic Rules:

  • Scoring: A match is usually played to the best of three games. Each game is played to 21 points (or 30 in some competitions).
  • Serving: The serve must be underhand and below the waist. The serve alternates between sides after each point.
  • Faults: A fault occurs when the shuttlecock hits the net or lands outside the court boundaries, or when a player touches the net with their body or racquet.
  • Winning a Point: A point is awarded to the side that wins a rally.
  • Winning a Game: The first side to reach 21 points (or 30) wins the game.

Why Badminton is Popular:

  • Fun and Easy to Learn: Badminton is relatively easy to pick up, making it enjoyable for players of all skill levels.
  • Great Exercise: It provides a full-body workout, improving cardiovascular fitness, agility, and coordination.
  • Social Sport: Badminton can be played socially or competitively, making it a great way to meet new people and make friends.
